Automatic Captions

Christine Sun Kim’s take on captions, and how they could be improved. What kind of information do regular captions usually convey? How can they be enhanced in order to actually inform (and inspire) Deaf viewers accessing content produced for normative mainstream channels?

Sound of Subtitles

[sound of subtitles] is a silent film by Seo Hye Lee, where the association of images and text invites the viewers to conjure their own interpretation of sounds and events.

UNHEARD
Unheard is a work by Ymer Marinus and Martina Raponi. The work is a multimedia multi sensorial interactive installation grounded in language. It uses a self-trained AI model connected to a technical apparatus that augments the experience of signing through different vibrational outputs and light effects.

Deaf Architecture

Deaf architecture centers the modes of interaction of Deaf communities, it not only foregrounds open spaces where visual contact is possible, but it also pays attention to adequate sound dampening and absorption, to minimize noise disruption in the functioning of hearing aids, for those individuals who choose to wear them.

Deaf Community

social media platforms, the possibility of sharing different kind of media, and instantaneous interaction through text and video, allowed Deaf communities to consolidate locally and internationally through groups, forums, etc.

social media platforms have seen an exponential growth of content created by Deaf creators, and that engages in different ways (e.g. in comedic reels, or memes) with the stigmatization of Deafhood and sign languages, trying to educate using the language and the forms of popular culture.

Deaf Poetry

Raymond Antrobus’ poem Signs, Music depicts the sonic and somatic experience of his son’s first signs, and his first approaches and engagements with the surrounding world.

Vero Leduc’s Dissonances is an example of Deaf Musicking, expressing the sonic through the embodiment and the articulation of signed poetry.

Internationalization

Turkoois

Organizations such as Turkoois, based in Groningen, promote Deaf culture and Sign Language on a local level, while actively engaging with organizations in other countries, to share resources, learn from each other, and strategize for forms of inclusivity that center the Deaf experience.

Mainstream Media

Coda

A film centering the Deaf experience in relation to the hearing world, through the complexity of familial relation. The performance of Deaf actor Troy Kotsur has been rewarded with the Best Supporting Actor Academy Award in 2022.

Sound Of Metal

This film, popularly cited in relation to Deafness and Deafhood points to the process of losing hearing, rather than centering the Deaf experience.

Sign Language

Sign Language is what distinguishes the Deaf in cultural and linguistic terms. The acknowledgment of Sign Languages' formal and grammatical aspects (only in the 1960s), their systematization, and their slow recognition as official languages in many countries (in some cases such as the Netherlands or Italy, the recognition happened only in 2020 and 2021 respectively) is of great importance for Deaf communities, their cultural activities, and for new models of inclusivity that center the Deaf perspective and language.

Spoken Language

Lip Reading and Oralism

The Second International Congress on the Education of the Deaf, held in Milan in 1880, became the symbol of linguistic and cultural oppression of the Deaf. The Congress’ deliberations were against Sign Language and in favor of Spoken Language and Lip Reading.

John lee Clark’s Distantism

John Lee Clark is a Deaf and Blind poet and writer, who came up with the term Distantism to describe all the forms of interaction (without touch, contact) that exclude DeafBlind individuals. He is also among the proponents of ProTactile, a language developed by DeafBlind individuals for DeafBlind individuals.

Alexander Graham Bell’s Ear Phonautograph (1874) utilized an excised human ear to transduce and visualize speech, so the Deaf could speak—or at least this is what Bell hoped. The device came to symbolize the repression of sign languages and the oppression of the Deaf, in favor of “oralism” (forcing the Deaf to speak and lipread).
